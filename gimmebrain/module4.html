Steps 1–3: Fields, Drive, & Photos
Close data, folder creation, and getting the pictures
5 min ▼

Step 1: Fill All Close Fields. Every custom field in the Opportunity must be filled before moving forward: address, ARV, repair estimate, realtor, purchase price, DD end date, market, property type, HVAC age, roof condition, electrical status, plumbing status, solar details (if any), pool status (if any). The Close Opportunity record is the source of truth.

Step 2: Create Google Drive Folder. Use your Good Results email account. Naming convention (required): [Street Address] - $[Price] - [Realtor Full Name]. Example: 123 Main Street - $125,500 - John Smith. Set access to "Anyone with the link → Viewer". Copy the sharing link and paste it into the Notes section of the opportunity in Close.

Step 3: Upload Property Photos. Photos are the priority. Buyers decide before visiting — clean photos signal organization. Request immediately from the realtor/seller. If they haven't provided: politely but firmly ask — "How can I make decisions without pictures at least?" If the temperature starts rising, let Gayden handle it. If still no photos, talk to Gayden — he may send a $40 runner. Never drive out yourself to take photos. Half a day lost = a deal missed.

Steps 4–5: Documents & Deal Page
Organizing contracts and building the deal page
4 min ▼

Step 4: Create Documents Subfolder. Inside the main Drive folder, create a subfolder named Documents. Upload the following: Purchase Agreement (RPA for Nevada, TREC contract for Texas), Assignment Agreement (if applicable), any amendments or supporting documents.

Step 5: Create Deal Page Using Deal Page Creator. Use the Deal Page Creator tool on the GR Hub. Enter the contract info, upload photos, and the tool builds the deal page, pushes data to Close, and creates the Google Drive automatically. The deal page includes:

Property Information: Address, Property Type (Single Family, Multi-Family, Condo, Apartment), Bedrooms, Bathrooms, Garage, Square Footage, Lot Size, Year Built

Contract Details: Contract Price, Close of Escrow Date, Due Diligence End Date, Earnest Money Amount and EMD Due Date, Contract Signed Date

Agent Information: Realtor Name, Phone Number, Email

Property Condition/Systems: HVAC condition, Roof condition, Foundation condition, Plumbing issues (if any), Electrical issues (if any), Pool condition/status (if applicable), Solar details: loan balance, remaining term, total payoff amount (if applicable)

Steps 6–7: Due Diligence & Access
Moving status and documenting property access
3 min ▼

Step 6: Move Opportunity to Due Diligence Period. Once the contract is signed and accepted, update the opportunity status in Close CRM to Due Diligence Period. This is a Won status. Moving here signals the deal is locked and disposition begins. A task is automatically created for Joe (he handles dispo).

Step 7: Document Access Information in Notes. In the Notes section of the opportunity in Close CRM, clearly document:

  • Exact access instructions for the property
  • Lockbox location and code (if applicable)
  • Who must be contacted for access and their contact information
  • Any restrictions on availability (specific days or times)

Include enough detail so access and property conditions are fully understood even if you are unavailable.

What Happens Next

Moving to Due Diligence Period triggers a task for Gayden. The disposition process begins immediately. You are encouraged to maintain communication with the seller/agent, but leadership takes over primary coordination from this point forward.
