Post-Contract SOP

The critical 24 hours after contract acceptance. Every step in order, same day. Close CRM fields, Google Drive organization, photos, documentation, and status moves. Execute this flawlessly.

Read Time~14 min

01 — Overview

Do Everything The Same Day

When a contract is signed and accepted, you enter the most critical 24 hours. Every step below must be done same day. Delays cascade failures through the entire operation.

Non-Negotiables

All steps must be completed same day or within 24 hours. If you're behind, ask for help. There is no flexibility here.

02 — Step 1: Fill All Close Fields

Step 1: Fill All Close Fields

The Close Opportunity record is the source of truth. Every custom field must be completed: address, ARV, repair estimate, realtor, purchase price, DD end date, market, property type, HVAC age, roof condition, electrical, plumbing, solar details.

+
Required Close CRM FieldsComplete list
▼

Core Fields: Official Offer Price, EMD Amount, Closing Costs, Close of Escrow Date, Property Address

Analysis Fields: ARV, ASK (asking price), MAO (maximum allowable offer), Current Contract Price

Timeline Fields: Due Diligence End Date, EMD Due Date, Contract Accepted Date

Property Systems: HVAC age and condition, roof age and condition, electrical condition, plumbing condition, solar (if applicable)

Deal Notes: Red flags, contingencies, unique issues — anything the team needs to know

03 — Step 2: Create Google Drive Folder

Step 2: Create Google Drive Folder

Create a shared folder using your Good Results email. This becomes the official deal repository for photos, contracts, and all deal documents.

Naming Convention
123 Main Street - $125,500 - John Smith

Set access to "Anyone with the link → Viewer" and paste the link into Close Notes immediately.

04 — Step 3: Upload Property Photos

Step 3: Upload Property Photos

Photos are the priority. Buyers decide before visiting. Get as many as possible. If realtor doesn't have them, ask the listing agent. If still unavailable, discuss with Gayden about a $40 runner service.

Critical Rule

Never drive out yourself to photograph. Lost time equals lost deals. The runner service exists for exactly this reason.

05 — Step 4: Create Documents Subfolder

Step 4: Create Documents Subfolder

Inside the main Drive folder, create a subfolder named Documents. Upload the signed contract and supporting docs here. Include the Purchase Agreement (RPA or TREC), Assignment Agreement if applicable, and any amendments.

06 — Step 5: Create Deal Basics Document

Step 5: Create Deal Basics Document

Create a Google Doc titled Deal Basics – [Property Address] in the main Drive folder. This is the quick-reference document for the entire team.

07 — Step 6: Move to Due Diligence Period

Step 6: Move to Due Diligence Period

Update the Opportunity status in Close to "Due Diligence Period". This is a Won status that signals the deal is locked. An automatic task fires for Gayden to coordinate contractors and title.

08 — Step 7: Document Access Information

Step 7: Document Access Information in Notes

In the Close Notes section, clearly document all access details. Someone on the team may need access without you present.

+
What to Document in NotesComplete list
▼

Lockbox: Location and code (if applicable)

Key Holder: Contact information and availability

Seller/Agent Availability: Times they are or are not available for access

Special Access Instructions: Alarm codes, gate codes, parking, any quirks with entry

Utilities: Status of water, electric, gas (on or off)

09 — What Happens Next

Gayden Takes Over

Once the opportunity moves to "Due Diligence Period," Gayden takes over coordination. A task automatically fires for him. Your job: stay available to the realtor and maintain relationship warmth. Gayden coordinates title, contractors, and closing inspections. You keep the realtor happy and responsive.
