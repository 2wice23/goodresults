01
High Motivation Signals
What triggers distressed seller urgency
2 min
▼

A distressed seller is under financial or emotional pressure. This pressure creates urgency—and urgency creates opportunity.

Key signals of a motivated seller:

  • Delinquent taxes or HOA fees
  • Property has been vacant or deteriorating
  • Timeline pressure (eviction, foreclosure date, probate closing deadline)
  • Financial stress (medical debt, job loss, divorce)
  • Holding costs eating into equity

High motivation is driven by timeline pressure and financial stress. The clock is ticking before the bank takes over, the tax sale happens, or probate drags on.

Certainty Over Price
Why a 21-day close beats 10% more in price
3 min
▼

This is the core principle of distressed deals: certainty and speed are worth more than a higher price.

A distressed seller will take a lower offer on a fast close because:

  • Every day costs money (property taxes, HOA, utilities, mortgage interest)
  • Holding a vacant property is risky (squatters, damage, weather)
  • They need relief NOW, not in 60 days
  • The longer the deal drags, the more reasons it can fall apart

A realtor might say: "The seller knows the retail retail would get $200K, but your $180K all-cash, as-is, 21-day close is worth way more because the pain stops immediately."

Certainty eliminates risk and ongoing holding costs.

Assignment Language Flexibility
How to handle seller objections to contract wording
4 min
▼

Many distressed sellers get nervous when they see assignment language in the contract. They worry the deal isn't real or they're being fooled.

Your approach: Be flexible on wording if it doesn't change the deal structure.

Example: If the seller says "I don't like the assignment clause," you can often say:

  • "We can remove the assignment language if you'd like."
  • "This is standard language that lets us work with partners, but if you're uncomfortable, we can simplify it."
  • "The assignment doesn't change what you're getting paid or when you close."

The key: Flexibility on wording while maintaining deal integrity is key to closing.

04
DOM & Price Signals
Reading market rejection as an opportunity
5 min
▼

DOM = Days on Market. This is the single best indicator of whether a property needs an investor or a retail buyer.

When you see a property with high DOM and recent price reductions, you're looking at an objective signal: The retail market rejected it.

Example:

  • Listed 4 months ago at $200K
  • Price dropped to $170K (15% reduction)
  • Still no retail offers

This is not a bad property—it's an investment opportunity because the retail market has spoken. The issue might be:

  • Cosmetic condition (buyer psychology)
  • Financed buyers won't qualify due to lender guidelines
  • Market condition (interest rates, seasonality)

High DOM with price reductions signal a motivated seller and investment opportunity.

05
Manufactured Homes & Specialized Assets
When to escalate and when to walk
3 min
▼

Manufactured homes (mobile homes on a pad) are a different animal. They have unique financing, title, and legal issues that don't apply to traditional houses.

If a distressed deal involves a manufactured home on a pad:

  • Don't assume standard real estate rules apply
  • Financing is harder to find
  • Title and ownership can be complicated
  • The property may be in a mobile home park with additional restrictions

When in doubt, escalate to Gayden. He has the specialized knowledge to evaluate these deals. Manufactured homes require specialized knowledge and should be escalated.

06
Executors & Probate Deals
Understanding fiduciary duty and motivation
3 min
▼

When a property is in probate, the executor (the person managing the estate) has a fiduciary duty—a legal obligation—to the heirs. This creates a unique motivation.

Why executors prefer a fast cash offer:

  • They have legal liability for the estate
  • Probate cases can drag on for months or years if the property sits unsold
  • They want to close the estate and get the heirs their money
  • They are personally responsible for property maintenance, taxes, and insurance during probate
  • A contingent offer that might fall through creates more risk and liability

Executors have fiduciary duty and legal liability. They prioritize closing over maximum proceeds.

07
Closing Timelines & Management
Building confidence through clear communication
3 min
▼

Distressed sellers are nervous. They've been burned before or they're in panic mode. A detailed closing timeline removes doubt and builds confidence.

What a good timeline looks like:

  • "We'll order the title search tomorrow"
  • "Title should be clear by Day 5"
  • "We'll order the appraisal on Day 2"
  • "Final walkthrough on Day 19"
  • "Closing at escrow on Day 21"

When a seller sees this timeline, they think: "Okay, this is real. They know what they're doing. I can trust this."

Clear timelines build confidence and remove objections before they become deal-killers.

08
Realtor Relationships & Pipeline
Building predictable deal flow through reliability
3 min
▼

A good realtor relationship is the lifeblood of your distressed deal pipeline. Realtors remember who closes and who flakes.

How to build loyalty:

  • Close on time, every time
  • Pay commissions promptly (don't make the realtor chase you)
  • Communicate clearly and often (realtors hate surprises)
  • Be transparent about deal terms
  • Follow up after closing to confirm commission was paid

When a realtor sends you a distressed deal, they're betting their reputation with their client on you. If you flake or close late, they'll never send you another deal.

Reliability and speed are how you build realtor relationships.

09
Title & Lien Research
Identifying deal-breakers early
3 min
▼

The moment a realtor sends you a distressed deal, your first job is to research the title and liens. This tells you whether the deal is viable.

Red flags to investigate:

  • Back taxes (state and federal)
  • HOA liens or unpaid assessments
  • Mortgage liens (first, second, third)
  • Mechanic liens from contractors
  • Judgment liens from lawsuits
  • Foreclosure notices or trustee's sales

Good news: Most liens get paid off at closing from the seller's proceeds in escrow. As long as there's equity to cover them, the deal still works.

Bad news: If liens exceed the property value, the seller has negative equity and may not be motivated to sell.

Financial stress signals (unpaid obligations, vacant holdings) show motivation.

10
Probate Timeline & Condition Issues
Why probate properties sit and decay
1 min
▼

Probate deals move slowly. Courts move slowly. During that time, a house sits vacant and deteriorates.

Why probate properties are often in poor condition:

  • The estate takes 6–12 months (sometimes 2+ years) to go through court
  • During this time, the house has no owner actively maintaining it
  • Utilities may be off, pipes may freeze, squatters may move in
  • Roof leaks, foundation issues, and HVAC problems go unfixed
  • By the time the executor can sell, it's a wreck

This is actually GOOD news for us: A beat-up probate property is exactly what Good Results specializes in.

Estates take time to process, during which maintenance is deferred.
