Our Position: Cash Buyers
Why cash is our non-negotiable competitive advantage
3 min ▼

Good Results is a cash buyer. That's the headline. Always. We buy with TRUE CASH from our bank account — no hard money, no private money, no borrowing from anyone. No lender permission needed. Cash. Period.

This is not a marketing angle. This is the operating principle. When you tell a realtor we're cash, you're telling them: contract signed → no appraisal, no financing contingency, no rate locks, no loan approval drama. We close in 10–21 days. That's the entire value prop.

The Golden Line

"One contract. One check. No tours. No mortgage approval drama. No appraisal fights. Fastest close they'll see all year."

When realtors work with us, they close faster and get paid without hand-holding. Our 10–21 day close (15-day average) is the proof. Position this every time a realtor asks about timeline or financing.

Texas Market Strategy
Repair-based MAO, cost tiers, and Spartan Title
4 min ▼

Texas (Houston metro) is lower acquisition cost with high renovation potential. Our MAO formula includes a repair deduction tied to scope. You estimate repair cost per square foot based on what the property needs.

MetricTexas Details
MarketHouston metro
Annual Volume~30 homes/year
MAO Formula(ARV × 75%) − Repair Estimate
Contract TypeTexas TREC (Residential Contract)
EscrowAaron Krominga — Spartan Title (aaronk@spartantitle.com)
Repair Cost Tiers

$20/sqft: Cosmetic only (paint, flooring, fixtures). $35/sqft: Typical full renovation (kitchen, bath, flooring, paint). $50–60/sqft: Heavy scope (roof, HVAC, electrical, plumbing).

Example: 2,000 sqft property with ARV $300k, needs standard reno (kitchen, bath). MAO = (300k × 0.75) − (2,000 × $35) = $225,000 − $70,000 = $155,000.

Spartan Title Strategy

Push Spartan Title on every Texas deal. We have a high-volume relationship with Aaron. If a seller already opened title elsewhere: offer to add their costs to your offer price instead of fighting.

MAO & Offer Pricing Philosophy
Never share the formula; always explain reasoning
3 min ▼

Your MAO (Maximum Acquisition Offer) is internal only. Never share it with realtors or sellers. They don't need to know your formula. They need to know: your offer is based on comps, repairs, and market conditions. That's transparent without revealing your math.

How to Explain Your Offer

"Comps in this market show $X–$Y. Based on the repairs needed (estimate: $Z/sqft for kitchen/bath/flooring), and current market conditions, I'm at $[offer price]."

This shows your work without exposing your ARV or profit margin. Realtors respect clarity. They don't respect "I just calculated it."

Never Say Wholesaler

Never use the word "wholesaler" with realtors. Wholesalers signal: no real capital, no commitment, middleman games. You're a buyer. Period.

Multi-Entity Structure & Volume Proof
Why multiple LLCs and how to position volume
2 min ▼

Good Results operates through multiple LLCs. This isn't complexity for complexity's sake. Multiple entities protect assets, manage liability, and provide flexibility in capital deployment across markets and deal types.

Realtors don't need to know this architecture. They see "Good Results" and care about: does this buyer have capital and will they close? Volume answers both.

Volume = Credibility

When you say "60 homes in Nevada this year," you're not bragging. You're providing proof. Volume is execution. It tells realtors you have the capital, the team, the process, and the certainty to close.

That 10–21 day average close time is only possible because we've done 60 deals. We know the markets, the title companies, the lenders' timelines, and the contractor networks. Volume is the proof of professionalism.
