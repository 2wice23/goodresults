Buy Box, Markets & Company

Understand the Good Results buy box criteria, market-specific MAO formulas (Nevada 69%, Texas 75%-repairs), title company relationships, and how to position yourself credibly to realtors as a cash buyer who closes fast.

01 — The Company

The Company

Good Results is Gayden Rosales and Joe. We buy distressed single-family homes cash and as-is. We have a construction company. The post-acquisition strategy depends on the deal: we might renovate and flip to retail buyers, convert to group homes, wholesale if margin is thin, or hold for cash flow. The strategy is determined by deal structure and property fit, never the plan going in. Rental holds only happen when a property doesn't sell fast enough — never the original plan.

Volume: ~60 homes per year in Nevada (combined Reno/Las Vegas). ~30 homes per year in Texas (Houston). Growing.

Entities: Multiple Good Results Texas LLC entities. Family trust for holds. Assignment clause in contracts so we can close under whichever entity has capital at closing.

Close timeline: 10–21 days. No appraisal. No financing contingency.

This context is what makes you credible on calls. When a realtor asks "How fast can you close?" you have a real answer: "We're a cash buyer. 10–21 days, no appraisal drama. We've done 60 homes in Nevada this year alone." That's not bragging—that's proof.

02 — Always Lead With Cash

Always Lead With Cash

Cash is the answer. Not hard money. Not private money. Not "we use a combination of sources." Just: cash.

Why? Because when realtors hear "hard money" or "private money," they know financing can fall through. When they hear "cash," they know the deal closes. That certainty is everything.

Standard Cash Buyer Answer
We're a cash buyer. We close in 10–21 days. No appraisal, no financing contingency, one check. That's what makes the deal easy for you.

What realtors have seen: Deals fall through on financing. Appraisal comes in low. Buyer walks. Realtor loses commission.

What we offer: One check. No financing drama. Commission paid on time.

Cash Positioning

Saying "cash buyer" instantly removes financing objections. It's the strongest positioning you have. Use it first, use it always.

03 — The Nevada Market

The Nevada Market

Markets: Reno/Sparks metro. Las Vegas/Henderson metro.

Property prices: Higher acquisition cost relative to Texas. More competitive market.

MAO Formula: 69% of ARV (no separate repair deduction in most cases)

Why 69% not 70%? Mental math. Gayden uses 69% as his default because it's easy to calculate fast and leaves room to undercut slightly if comps are close. If a property has serious issues (foundation cracks, roof, major electrical/plumbing), discuss with Gayden before offering—he may go lower.

Contract type: Nevada RPA.

Nevada Formula

MAO = ARV × 0.69. No separate repair deduction for most properties.

04 — The Texas Market

The Texas Market

Market: Houston metro.

Property prices: Cheaper real estate. Higher renovation potential.

MAO Formula: 75% of ARV minus Repair Estimate

Repair Estimate = square footage × cost per square foot (your discretion, $20–$60/sqft)

Repair Tier$/sqftScope
Cosmetic$20Paint, flooring, minor fixtures
Standard$35Kitchen, bathrooms, floors, paint throughout
Heavy$50Roof, HVAC, electrical, plumbing
Everything$60Roof + HVAC + electrical + plumbing + structural

Example: ARV $280,000. Property is 1,800 sqft. Standard renovation (kitchen, bath, flooring, paint).

MAO = (280,000 × 0.75) − (1,800 × $35) = 210,000 − 63,000 = $147,000

Contract type: Texas TREC.

05 — The Construction Angle & Title Companies

The Construction Angle & Title Companies

When a realtor questions why you need your own title company or why you don't walk properties pre-contract, use the construction company story.

Construction Angle Explanation
I own a construction company. I go through about 60 homes a year between Nevada and Texas. I have contractors, roofers, electricians, plumbers—I need to be in contract to send them. That's just how my business model works.

This explanation is honest and builds credibility. It explains why your process looks different from typical buyers.

Title Companies:

Texas: Spartan Title (Aaron is our contact). We have a high-volume relationship with them. Push to use Spartan on every Texas deal.

Nevada: Landmark Title (Jenine), Core Title (Amy), Spartan Title (Aaron for overlap). Contact Gayden for current preferred provider on Nevada deals.

If a seller has already opened title with another company: Don't fight it. Add their title costs to your offer price. "No problem. We'll add those costs to our offer and you can stay with your current title company." This keeps the deal alive.

What is a "hard money" loan and why don't we mention it? 3 min read
▼

Hard money is short-term financing from private lenders. It's faster than bank loans but comes with higher interest rates (10–15%+) and points (2–5% upfront). Hard money lenders don't care about credit or income; they care about property value and LTV (loan-to-value ratio).

Why don't we mention it? Because realtors assume hard money borrowers might still back out if the appraisal comes in low or if the deal doesn't pencil. They've seen deals die on hard money because the borrower couldn't secure funds or walked away.

We say "cash" because we have the funds. No appraisal risk. No financing contingency. It's simpler and more credible to a realtor than explaining our capital structure. The source of those funds (hard money, private money, our own capital) doesn't matter to the realtor. What matters is: you're buying, you're closing, the check clears.

+
Why does Gayden have multiple LLCs? 2 min read
▼

Multiple entities protect assets, manage liability, and allow flexibility in capital deployment. Different LLCs can hold properties in different stages (acquisition, rehab, hold), finance different projects, or operate in different states with different tax structures.

From the realtor's perspective, it doesn't matter. They see "Good Results" on the contract and know the deal closes. The LLC name is a legal detail we manage internally. Some contracts close under "Good Results Texas LLC," some under another entity, some under the family trust. All that matters to the realtor is: funds available, deal closes, commission paid.

The assignment clause in our contracts allows us flexibility to close under whichever entity has capital at the time of closing. This is standard and protects our operation.

06 — Forbidden Language

Forbidden Language — Never Say These Things

Never use these terms with realtors or sellers:

  • "Wholesaler" — Realtors assume you're a middleman with no real capital. You're a buyer, not a flipper or wholesaler.
  • "Hard money" — Signals financing risk. Always say "cash."
  • "MAO" — This is internal. Never mention your formula. Say "offer price" based on comps and repairs.
  • "Repair estimate is just rough" — Sound confident. You've walked 100 properties. You know the numbers.
  • "We might wholesale this" — If margin is thin, you'll mention it, but only after you have it under contract. Never pre-offer as a wholesale.
Words Matter

Realtors make snap judgments based on language. "Cash buyer" sounds different from "wholesaler." Use the right words and you close more deals.

07 — Value Prop to Realtors

Your Value Proposition

When a realtor asks "Why should I work with you?", here's what makes you different:

Realtor Value Prop
We're a cash buyer. No financing drama, no appraisal gaps, no contingencies. We close in 10–21 days. We've done 60 homes this year. You list it, we buy it, your commission is paid on time. That's it.

Your positioning stack (in order of importance):

  • Cash buyer (no financing risk)
  • Fast close (10–21 days)
  • No appraisal (no appraisal gap risk)
  • Volume proof (60 homes/year)
  • Easy commission (one check, on time)
