The Goal of a Cold Call
What you're actually trying to accomplish
2 min ▼

The goal of a cold call is to get them to trust you enough to send you a deal. That's it. Don't pitch too hard. Don't explain everything. Make them feel heard.

A good call gets the realtor comfortable talking to you, asking questions, and thinking about properties they might send. Realtors work with many buyers — cash buyers, fix-and-flippers, owner-occupants, wholesalers. Your job is to become the go-to investor in your market by showing up, being easy to work with, and closing consistently.

The metric that matters: 100 calls per day is the target. In training, 50+ calls per day is acceptable. This is a phone-call-heavy business. Texts open pathways to calls, but calls get deals.

Opening — The Three-Step Structure
How every cold call starts — name, company, qualifying question
3 min ▼

Every cold call follows the same structure. Realtors are busy — they need to know who you are and why they should stay on the phone in the first 15 seconds.

StepWhat to Do
1. State Your Name & CompanyClear, confident. "Hey, this is [Name] with Good Results."
2. Ask a Qualifying Question"Do you ever get off-market properties or investor-friendly stuff?" This filters for yes/no quickly.
3. If Yes → Deliver Buy Box | If No → Permission to Follow UpIf they say yes, deliver the buy box. If no, get permission to follow up and move on to the next call.
Sample Opener

"Hey, is this [Name]? Hey, what's up — it's [Your Name] with Good Results. Quick question — do you ever get off-market properties or investor-friendly stuff?"

The Buy Box Pitch (30 Seconds)
What to say when they say yes
2 min ▼

Delivery is everything. This is your competitive edge. Say it warm, confident, and fast.

The Buy Box (Verbatim)

"We're cash buyers. We close as fast as possible — usually 10 to 21 days — no bank, no appraisal, no conditions. We buy as-is — any condition. We're in [market]. One contract, one check."

Key points to hit:

• Cash buyer (always lead with this)
• 10–21 day close (as fast as possible)
• As-is, any condition
• No appraisal contingency, no contingencies at all
• Easy relationship (no tours, no hand-holding)

Do NOT proactively mention commission. If they ask, confirm it — don't lead with it. Realtors already know you pay commission. Leading with "We pay 2.5-3%" signals weakness.

Making Realtors Feel Seen
The #1 skill for repeat deals and warmth
2 min ▼

This is the #1 skill that turns one-off calls into repeat deal sources. Realtors who feel understood bring deals. Realtors who feel like a transaction number go cold immediately.

Key tactics:

• Remember something from the last call and reference it: "Hey, how did that listing go?" Shows you paid attention.

• Schedule a follow-up text 3 days after a good call: Just "Hey, hope your week's going well" — no pitch. Keeps you warm in their mind.

• If they mention something personal (dog, trip, stressful listing), acknowledge it. It costs nothing. "I hope you get a million bucks this weekend for you and your sellers. If not, I'll hit you up Tuesday."

Consistency in personality also matters. If you're warm on call 1 and suddenly cold on call 5, realtors notice. That kills trust.

Power Dialer & Calling Through a List
How to hit 100 calls/day efficiently
3 min ▼

Power dialer is your tool for volume. It sequences calls and logs them, so you're not manually dialing each number or writing notes for every call. Here's the workflow:

StepWhat to Do
1. Pick a ListUse a smart view like "Reno Warm" or "Houston Warm" — these are pre-built lists of targeted realtors.
2. Start Power DialerHit the phone icon at the top to start the dialer. Set your outbound number before the first call.
3. Hit the Calls/Hour TargetGoal: 10 calls per hour minimum when starting. Quality comes with reps — don't obsess over perfection on call 1.
4. Log Every CallAfter every call, log in Close. One sentence is enough. Update the Lead Stage if conversation warrants it.

As a new agent, take every meaningful call to the Call Analyzer. This is the fastest way to improve. The analyzer reviews the call, pulls positive patterns (did you deliver the buy box?), flags negative patterns (did you ask "are you still a realtor?"), and gives you coaching notes. Once you're experienced and comfortable, this becomes less critical — but early on, it's how you build skill fast.

Common Objections & Winning Responses
Scripts for the most common pushback
4 min ▼

These objections come up repeatedly. The key to all responses: acknowledge their concern first, then explain your process. Never argue. Never make them wrong.

ObjectionResponse
"I need you to walk the property first""I'm not a contractor. I pay someone to hang pictures at my house. My contractors charge $600–800 a day. I need to be in contract before I can send contractors. If we agree on price, I get you a 5–7 day due diligence and my guys will be there next day."
"My seller wants a higher price""I totally get it. I hope they get it. Let me know if it doesn't work out — I'll be here." (Don't chase. Don't argue. Stay warm.)
"I only work with owner-occupants"Acknowledge, get permission to reach out if they get something investor-friendly, and move on. No point fighting this.
"What's your offer based on?"Share your reasoning (ARV, repair estimate, market data). Never share MAO — it telegraphs your ceiling.
"I've been burned by investors before""I completely understand — and I don't want to waste your time either. Here's how we work: you bring me a deal, I run my numbers, and then I'll just ask you to verbally float the price to your seller. If they come back and say they need more, no problem — no contract gets written. But if they're close, we move forward. No wasted paperwork unless it's worth it for everyone."

Float the Verbal & Scoring Patterns
How to avoid deals dying on paper and what the Call Analyzer is looking for
3 min ▼

Before writing any contract, always float the verbal first. This is non-negotiable.

"Based on what I'm seeing, I'm thinking somewhere around X. Does that work for your seller?"

If they say no and won't budge — don't write the contract. Save everyone's time. No wasted paperwork unless it's worth it for everyone.

Negative patterns the Call Analyzer flags:

• Asking "are you still a realtor?" — wastes time, confuses people, triggers suspicion
• Using "wholesale," "wholesaler," "assign," or "flip/fix and flip" in COLD texts
• Saying "just moved here" or "just got here" — undermines credibility
• Saying "took time off" or "took a break" — undermines credibility
• Saying "me and my partner are trying to do..." — sounds unestablished
• Answering your own questions or giving easy outs
• Ending with "keep me posted" as the only next step — passive
• Not delivering the buy box when the realtor has nothing — missed opportunity
• Punting to email when a realtor says they have properties — dig in on the call

Positive patterns you want to build:

• State your name clearly in first few seconds
• Reference prior conversation with specific detail (month, topic, property)
• Deliver buy box verbally on every call, even short ones
• Differentiate from other investors ("I'm not just going to submit an offer for fun")
• Run comps live on the phone
• Float verbal numbers before any contract
• Set specific follow-up timeline ("Can I check back in two weeks?")
• Ask "on market or off market?" as a qualifying question
• Tell realtor to save your number with a label ("Save me as [Name], investor")
• Let the realtor talk and ask follow-up questions about their deals
• Keep tone warm, casual, low-pressure

The Conversion Math & Consistency
Why volume and consistency matter more than perfection
2 min ▼

Here's the math that drives the business:

100 calls/day × 1-2% conversion rate = 1-2 deals/day

That 1-2% conversion is standard across the industry. It's not because you're not good enough — it's because most realtors don't send properties, most properties aren't in your buy box, and most sellers don't accept offers. You're playing a numbers game.

Volume + Consistency = Results

The realtors who send the most deals are the ones you've called 10+ times. Consistency signals reliability. Realtors notice when you change personality based on deal flow. Stay warm, stay available, and deals come back to you.

In the early days, aim for 50+ calls/day. As you scale, push to 100. Quality call-handling comes with reps, not perfection on day 1.
