How Close CRM Works
Leads, opportunities, and the golden rule of logging
2 min▼

Our CRM is Close. Everything lives here — every realtor, every property, every offer, every follow-up. If it's not in Close, it doesn't exist.

The structure is simple: a Lead is a person (realtor or seller). An Opportunity is a property deal attached to that lead. One realtor can have multiple opportunities — one per property they bring you.

The Golden Rule

Log every call in Close immediately after it ends. Update the lead status the moment the situation changes. A stale pipeline is a broken pipeline.

Close also powers your automated workflows — offer emails, follow-up tasks, SMS sequences. These only fire correctly if the data in the CRM is accurate. Garbage in, garbage out.

Property Comp Pipeline
Intake, evaluation, and the path from Unprocessed to Approved
4 min▼

Every new deal opportunity starts in the Property Comp Pipeline. This pipeline is for evaluating whether a property is worth pursuing. You create opportunities here, Joe reviews them, and winning ones flow automatically into the Properties Pipeline.

How to Create an Opportunity

Use the "Create Opportunity" Custom Activity button on the lead's profile — NOT the standard "+ New Opportunity." This is critical. Using the wrong button skips the automation and breaks the workflow.

StatusWhat Happens
UnprocessedOpportunity just created. A 2-hour task auto-fires for you to run a soft comp (quick Zillow check). Worth pursuing → "Run Me." Not a deal → Dead Ass.
Run MeWorth a full comp. Joe receives a task to run detailed comps and provide pricing feedback. Don't move here unless you've verified it's worth his time.
ApprovedJoe confirmed it qualifies. An automated workflow immediately creates a matching opportunity in the Properties Pipeline at "Pre Twork 💦." You do NOT manually duplicate.
Dead AssJoe determined it doesn't qualify. If price issue → twork at Joe's numbers. If bad for another reason → Dead. Either way, contact the realtor and explain.

Properties Pipeline
Active deal management from Pre Twork to Chicken Dinner
5 min▼

This is where the real work happens. Once Joe approves a deal, it lands here at "Pre Twork 💦" and you run it from first contact through closing. Every status has an automated workflow behind it — the accuracy of your status moves directly determines whether the right tasks and messages fire.

StatusWhat Happens
Pre Twork 💦Auto-created when approved. Task fires: review property, confirm numbers with Joe, call the realtor to assess if a deal can happen now.
Tworking ItDeal can't happen now. Day 6: automated SMS for updates. Day 7: follow-up task created. Continue every 1–3 weeks.
Send TermsReady to submit an offer. Fill ALL required fields first. Triggers: pre-filled offer email, SMS to realtor, next-day follow-up task.
Offer OutSigned contract sent back. Gayden/Joe moves it here. Automated SMS confirms, 24-hour follow-up task fires.
Due DiligenceOffer accepted. Deal locked. Automatic task fires for Joe (dispo). Start Post-Contract SOP immediately.
A$$ignedSuccessfully assigned to end buyer. Leadership manages.
Chicken Dinner 🍗Fully closed and paid. Money in the door. This is the goal.
Dead Ass / CanceledFell through or canceled. Always document the reason.

Creating an Opportunity
What to collect on the call and why the button matters
3 min▼

When you're on a call with a realtor and a property sounds promising, create the opportunity immediately using the Custom Activity button. Collect these details while you have them on the phone:

StepWhat to Collect
1. Basic InfoProperty address and asking price — required to create the opportunity
2. MechanicalsHVAC age/condition, electrical status, plumbing status — these affect repair cost estimates
3. SolarIf applicable: loan balance, payoff amount, monthly payment. Outstanding solar loans can kill a deal.
4. PoolIf applicable: pool status. Pools add cost and complexity to renovation estimates.

The Custom Activity button triggers a chain of automated actions: correct pipeline placement, "Unprocessed" starting status, and the 2-hour soft comp task. The standard button skips all of this. Always use the Custom Activity button.

Required Fields Before Send Terms
The five fields that make or break your offer email
3 min▼

These fields feed directly into the offer email templates. If any are blank, the email will break — it'll send with empty placeholders instead of real data. Fill them all before moving to Send Terms.

Required — Fill Before Send Terms

Official Offer Price · EMD Amount · Closing Costs · COE Date · Address

Additional fields to complete when available: ARV, ASK, MAO, Current Contract Price, Wholesale Price, DD End Date, EMD Due Date, Accepted Date, On Market status.

Once a deal moves to Due Diligence Period, you must also add: Current Contract Price, COE Date, DD End Date, EMD Amount, EMD Due Date, and Accepted Date. These are used by leadership and title.

Daily Habits & Workflow
The five CRM habits that keep your pipeline alive
3 min▼

A CRM is only as good as the habits behind it. Here's the daily workflow that keeps your pipeline clean and your automations working:

StepWhat to Do
1New realtor enters system: Assign yourself as Lead Owner. Set a follow-up task for 1 week: "First Follow-Up."
2After every call: Log the call in Close and update the Lead Stage immediately. Don't let calls pile up.
3When deal moves to Send Terms: Fill ALL required custom fields before changing status. Moving first breaks the offer email.
4Fully signed contract received: Go to the Deal Processing page — enter the contract, build the Google Drive, upload pictures/documents, share drive via the opportunity notes.
5Contract sent to YOUR email for signing: Tell realtor to send to contracts@goodresults.org. Verify they entered all info from your offer terms correctly.

Why logging matters: The next person who opens that lead has no idea what was said if you didn't log it. Smart views, follow-up tasks, and automated sequences all depend on accurate data. Log every call — it takes 30 seconds.
