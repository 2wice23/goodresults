01 — How Close CRM Works

How Close CRM Works

Our CRM is Close. Everything lives here — every realtor, every property, every offer, every follow-up. If it's not in Close, it doesn't exist.

The structure is simple: a Lead is a person (realtor or seller). An Opportunity is a property deal attached to that lead. One realtor can have multiple opportunities — one per property they bring you.

The Golden Rule

Log every call in Close immediately after it ends. Update the lead status the moment the situation changes. A stale pipeline is a broken pipeline.

Close also powers your automated workflows — offer emails, follow-up tasks, SMS sequences. These only fire correctly if the data in the CRM is accurate. Garbage in, garbage out.

Don't Let Statuses Rot

Move the status the moment the situation changes. If someone answered a call they're no longer "Attempting Contact" — they're "Connected." Wrong statuses break smart views and make your pipeline fiction instead of fact.

03 — Property Comp Pipeline

Property Comp Pipeline (Intake & Evaluation)

Every new deal opportunity starts in the Property Comp Pipeline. This pipeline is for evaluating whether a property is worth pursuing. You create opportunities here, Joe reviews them, and winning ones flow automatically into the Properties Pipeline.

How to Create an Opportunity

Use the "Create Opportunity" Custom Activity button on the lead's profile — NOT the standard "+ New Opportunity." This is critical. Using the wrong button skips the automation and breaks the workflow.

+
Property Comp Pipeline Statuses Intake flow
▼

Unprocessed: Opportunity just created. A 2-hour task auto-fires for you to run a soft comp (quick Zillow check). If it looks worth pursuing → move to "Run Me." If it's clearly not a deal → move to Disapproved.

Run Me: You've determined the property is worth a full comp. Joe receives a task to run detailed comps and provide pricing feedback. Do not move here unless you've already verified it's worth his time.

Approved: Joe confirmed the property qualifies. An automated workflow immediately creates a matching opportunity in the Properties Pipeline at "Pre Twork 💦." You do NOT manually duplicate this.

Disapproved: Joe determined it doesn't qualify — numbers don't work, outside buy box, etc. Your job: contact the realtor and explain why. We respect their time and keep the relationship warm.

04 — Properties Pipeline

Properties Pipeline (Active Deal Management)

This is where the real work happens. Once Joe approves a deal, it lands here at "Pre Twork 💦" and you run it from first contact through closing. Every status has an automated workflow behind it — which means the accuracy of your status moves directly determines whether the right tasks and messages fire.

+
Full Properties Pipeline Statuses All stages from intake to close
▼

Pre Twork 💦: Auto-created when a deal is approved. A task fires: review property details, confirm numbers with Joe, and call the realtor to assess if a deal can happen now. This is your opening move.

Tworking It: Deal can't happen right now — price gap, seller not ready, timing issue. A workflow fires: Day 6 sends an automated SMS asking for updates, Day 7 creates a follow-up task. You continue following up every 1–3 weeks.

Send Terms: You're ready to submit an offer. Before moving here, complete ALL required custom fields — they feed directly into the offer email. Moving here triggers: (1) pre-filled offer email draft, (2) SMS to realtor confirming terms, (3) next-day follow-up task.

Offer Out: Signed contract has been sent back. Gayden or Joe moves the opportunity here after signing. An automated SMS confirms completion, and a 24-hour follow-up task fires.

Due Diligence Period: Offer accepted. Deal is locked. An automatic task fires for Gayden. You immediately begin the Post-Contract SOP and disposition starts. The deal is real — protect it.

A$$igned: Deal successfully assigned to an end buyer. Leadership manages this.

Chicken Dinner: Deal fully closed and paid. Money in the door. This is the goal.

Dead / Canceled Contract: Opportunity fell through or signed contract was canceled. Always document the reason.

06 — Creating an Opportunity

Creating an Opportunity — What to Collect

When you're on a call with a realtor and a property sounds promising, create the opportunity immediately using the Custom Activity button. Collect these details while you have them on the phone.

+
Key Data to Collect When Creating an Opportunity Four critical fields
▼

Step 1 — Basic Property Info: Get the address and their asking price. These are required fields — you can't create the opportunity without them.

Step 2 — Mechanical Condition: Ask about HVAC age/condition, electrical status, and plumbing status. These affect repair cost estimates significantly.

Step 3 — Solar (if applicable): Get the full finance info — loan balance, payoff amount, and monthly payment. Solar with an outstanding loan can kill a deal.

Step 4 — Pool (if applicable): Note the pool status. Pools add cost and complexity to renovation estimates.

+
Why the Custom Activity button matters Deep dive

Close CRM has two ways to create an opportunity: the standard "+ New Opportunity" button and the "Create Opportunity" Custom Activity button. They look similar, but they're completely different.

The Custom Activity button triggers a chain of automated actions: it places the opportunity into the correct pipeline, sets the starting status to "Unprocessed," and fires the 2-hour soft comp task. None of that happens with the standard button.

Using the wrong button means Joe doesn't get notified, the task doesn't fire, the opportunity may land in the wrong pipeline, and the whole follow-up chain gets broken. Always use the Custom Activity button.

07 — Required Fields Before Send Terms

Required Fields Before Moving to Send Terms

These fields feed directly into the offer email templates. If any are blank, the email will break — it'll send with empty placeholders instead of real data. Fill them all before moving to Send Terms.

Required — Fill Before Send Terms

Official Offer Price · EMD Amount · Closing Costs · COE Date · Address

If any of these are blank, the offer email will go out with broken placeholders. Check every field before you move the status.

Additional fields to complete when available: ARV (After Repair Value), ASK (Seller's asking price), MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer), Current Contract Price, Dates (DD End Date, EMD Due Date, Accepted Date), On Market status.

Once a deal moves to Due Diligence Period, you must also add: Current Contract Price, COE Date, DD End Date, EMD Amount, EMD Due Date, and Accepted Date. These are used by leadership and title.

09 — Daily Habits & Workflow

Working the System — Day-to-Day Habits

A CRM is only as good as the habits behind it. Here's the daily workflow that keeps your pipeline clean and your automations working.

+
Five Daily CRM Habits Workflow checklist
▼

Step 1 — New realtor enters system: Assign yourself as Lead Owner immediately. Set a follow-up task for 1 week: "First Follow-Up."

Step 2 — After every call: Log the call in Close. Update the Lead Stage immediately. Don't let calls pile up — log them while details are fresh.

Step 3 — When deal moves to Send Terms: Fill ALL remaining required custom fields before changing the status. Moving first and filling later breaks the offer email.

Step 4 — Contracts received: All contracts sent to you → forward immediately to contracts@goodresults.org. Both Gayden and Joe receive it. This prevents lost contracts.

Step 5 — When opportunity hits Due Diligence: Fill the full set of DD fields (Contract Price, COE Date, DD End Date, EMD details, Accepted Date) and immediately begin the Post-Contract SOP checklist (Module 4).

+
What happens if I skip logging a call? Why it matters

Short answer: the next person who opens that lead — whether it's you tomorrow, or Joe, or Gayden — has no idea what was said. They can't pick up where you left off. They might call the same realtor with the same pitch you already gave.

Long answer: the smart views, follow-up tasks, and automated sequences all depend on accurate data. If the Last Contact Date is stale, you'll appear in the wrong smart views. If the status is wrong, tasks won't fire at the right time.

Log every call. It takes 30 seconds. Do it before you dial the next number.
