Investor Vocabulary

Master the language of funding. Know what to say to realtors, understand the mechanics behind the scenes, and handle the nuances of different financing types — from cash and hard money to owner financing and manufactured home deals.

Always Lead With Cash
Your opening positioning on every call
2 min ▼

When any realtor, seller, or agent asks how you fund deals, your answer is simple and consistent: "We're a cash buyer. We close in 10–21 days, no bank approval needed, no appraisal contingency." Don't elaborate unless directly pressed. Good Results buys with TRUE CASH from our bank account. Period. No hard money. No private money. No borrowing from anyone. No lender permission needed.

This isn't deception. It's positioning. The mechanisms underneath don't matter to the realtor. What matters is whether the deal closes, on time, without contingencies. That's what "cash" signals.

The Golden Rule

Lead with certainty. Realtors have been burned by "cash buyers" who fell through. Your job is to signal strength and follow-through, every single time.

Hard Money: Speed vs. Cost
Why we use it and when it makes sense
3 min ▼

Hard Money: Short-term, asset-backed loans from private lenders — typically 6–18 months at high interest rates (10–15% annually). Hard money lenders lend based on property value, not borrower credit. The lender evaluates your exit strategy, not your credit score.

Why we use it: When we need to close fast without waiting for traditional bank approval (which takes 30–45 days), hard money lets us fund the deal in days. It costs more in interest, but it closes like cash to the seller. We never volunteer this info to the realtor — they don't care about the mechanism, only that we close.

Critical Point

Never lead with "hard money" to a realtor. It raises questions about capital stability and triggers red flags. Say "cash," not the mechanism.

Owner Financing & Private Money
When the seller acts as the bank; internal capital sources
3 min ▼

Owner Financing (Seller Financing): The seller carries the note — the buyer makes monthly payments directly to the seller instead of a bank. Used when a seller owns the property free and clear and is willing to act as the bank. Can be attractive to sellers who want monthly income. Not something we do on acquisitions, but may come up when we're selling a property to an end buyer.

Private Money: Loans from individuals (friends, family, high-net-worth investors) rather than institutions. Lower rates than hard money, more flexible terms, but still involves a lender relationship. We are NOT private money. When an investor says "I use private money," they have a partner or backer providing funds on a deal-by-deal basis. Our capital is ours — no partner approval, no draw schedule, no lender.

Owner Finance Advantage

Motivated sellers with free-and-clear properties may prefer monthly payments (tax spread across years) over a lump sum. This is especially valuable for manufactured home portfolio deals.

Conventional Loans vs. Your Positioning
What retail buyers use (and why you're different)
2 min ▼

Conventional Loan: A standard bank mortgage. Requires credit score, income verification, appraisal, and typically takes 30–45 days to close. Sellers hate the uncertainty. This is what retail buyers use. Completely irrelevant to our acquisitions — but know it so you can position against it.

When a realtor mentions a buyer with a conventional loan, your opening is always: "We're not a conventional buyer. No appraisal. No bank. We close in 10–21 days." This is your competitive advantage. Many realtors have been burned by conventional buyers whose loans fell through. You're the anti-conventional buyer.

Manufactured Homes: Specialty Financing
Limited buyer pool and strict requirements
4 min ▼

Multi-moved manufactured homes (moved 2+ times) have a very limited buyer pool because standard financing (FHA/conventional) is not available. Only specialty lenders (21st Mortgage Corporation, Credit Human, Triad) will touch them, and their requirements are strict:

RequirementSpecification
Minimum Credit Score660 — no exceptions for lower credit
Down Payment5% of borrower's own funds
Interest Rate~8% — higher than conventional
Loan Term25 years — standard for specialty lenders

The buyer pool that qualifies: someone with $50K–$80K in savings, 660+ credit, stable income. That's not the pool buying mobile homes. This creates a very small buyer pool.

When Realtors Doubt Your Funding
Building credibility and closing confidence
2 min ▼

Realtors have been burned. They've had a "cash buyer" come in, get a deal under contract, and then reveal financing contingencies mid-closing. The deal falls apart. The realtor looks bad. Their client is angry. Realtors remember this.

When you say cash, you're making a promise. Your entire credibility rests on follow-through. Every deal that closes on time, without contingency surprises, creates a realtor who will send you more deals. Your reputation is your most valuable asset in this business.

If a realtor presses you for proof: "Call Aaron at my title company — he'll tell you exactly what our track record looks like. We close deals on time, period." Third-party credibility beats your word every time.

DSCR & Other Investment Financing
What you'll hear from other buyers
2 min ▼

DSCR Loan (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): A type of investment property loan where qualification is based on the rental income the property generates, not the borrower's personal income. Used by landlords and long-term hold investors. Not relevant to our acquisitions. You may hear it from buyers on the dispo side or from other investors talking about their long-term portfolios.

When you hear "DSCR," you'll know someone is a landlord/hold investor, not a cash buyer. This isn't your competition — they're playing a different game with different timelines and capital structures. You're the speed and certainty play.
