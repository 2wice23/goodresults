SMS Playbook

Master cold and warm SMS outreach. Learn forbidden words, templates, cadences, and proven patterns from closed deals. SMS gets realtors on the phone—texts are the opener, not the closer.

01 — Why SMS Works

Realtors Ignore Calls and Email

A casual, human-feeling text gets read. Email disappears. Calls go to voicemail. But a text that feels like it's from a real person, not a company, gets a response. The goal of cold SMS is simple: get them on the phone. Once you're on a call, you pitch. SMS is the opener, not the closer.

02 — Forbidden Words in SMS

NEVER Use These Words in Text

These words trigger red flags and spam filters. They signal you're trying to pass a contract without closing.

FULL FORBIDDEN LIST

NEVER in first cold SMS: "wholesaler", "wholesaling", "wholesale", "assign", "assigning", "assignment", "flip", "fix and flip", "cash buyer", "investor"

OK in follow-up SMS and calls: "fix and flip", "cash buyer", and "investor" are acceptable once a relationship is established. The restriction above applies to your very first cold outreach text only.

Why the distinction? In that first cold SMS, these words broadcast exactly what you are before you've earned trust. On a live call or in follow-up texts after the relationship is warm, tone and context matter — "fix and flip" and "cash buyer" are fine.

03 — Cold SMS Rules

Short, Human, One Question

Cold SMS has strict rules. Violate them and the text feels like a bot or a spam blast.

RuleWhy It Matters
Keep it shortOne or two lines max. If the realtor needs to scroll, you lost.
Write like a real personUse casual language and occasional emoji. Signals a real human, not a company blast.
Never reveal full pitchSave it for the call. SMS is just the door opener.
One question per textMultiple questions feel like a survey and trigger decline rates.
Goal: get a yes or replyOnce they respond, you've won. Now get them on the phone.
04 — Cold SMS Opening Examples

Three Templates That Work

Opening Example 1
Hey [Name], do you ever work with buyers looking for fixer properties? 👋
Opening Example 2
Hey, you still a realtor? Got a program if you ever get investor-friendly stuff.
Opening Example 3
Hey [Name] — quick question, do you get off-market listings in [area]?

After they reply "Who is this?" — you've won. They're engaged. Now get them on the phone and pitch.

05 — Post-Call SMS (Mandatory)

Send the "0 Great Talk - Buy Box" Template Same Day

After every live conversation where the realtor showed interest, send this template same day while the call is fresh. Do not wait until the next morning. This is the gold standard post-call text.

The template includes the buy box in text form, your name, Good Results, and keeps their number saved. It's mandatory because it sets you apart from other investors who ghost after a call.

06 — Two Different Follow-Up Cadences

Know the Difference

Post-call texts and cold outreach cadences are completely different. Do not confuse them.

Cadence TypeTimingPurpose
Post-Call Text3 days after callWarm relationship touch. "Hey [Name], hope the week's treating you well." Not a pitch.
Cold Outreach18–21 days apartFor realtors who haven't responded. Standard spacing for cold or silent leads.
CRITICAL DISTINCTION

The 3-day post-call text is a relationship move after a live conversation. The 18–21 day cadence is for cold or unresponsive realtors. These are NOT the same. Do not mix them.

09 — Proven Patterns From Closed Deals

Five Patterns That Work

+
Pattern 1: Playful Escalation Sequence
▼

Each text raises emotional stakes slightly without turning aggressive:

1. Friendly check-in: "How goes it [Name]? I hope '26 is off to a killer start for you... Any fresh listings current or coming up I can check out this week?"

2. Playful guilt trip: "Come onnnnn lol... Is this your way of saying I'm not your favorite anymore?"

3. Humor/fake hurt: "Well shoot... Just tell me you hate me if you want to ignore me 🥲"

4. Light persistence: "I'm not trying to be annoying, it's just the squeaky wheel gets the grease lol."

Why it works: Escalation without aggression. The realtor is entertained and engaged.

+
Pattern 2: "Help Me Spend Money"
▼

This exact phrase appears across multiple closed deals:

"How's my very favorite realtor in the whole wide world doing? Help me spend some money before the end of [month] lol 🙏"

Why it works: It's casual, funny, creates time urgency without pressure, and reframes the relationship. The realtor is doing YOU a favor by bringing you deals.

+
Pattern 3: Holiday and Personal Messages
▼

Christmas messages, "hope you had a great break" texts, and personal check-ins appear in every closed deal thread. These aren't deal-seeking texts—they're pure relationship maintenance.

Example: "Heyyyy [Name]! I just wanted to reach out and wish you a Happy Christmas and a Merry New Year.. I hope you're having the best time with your family"

These keep you top of mind during periods when there's no active deal.

+
Pattern 4: Post-Deal Re-Engagement
▼

After a deal closes, successful threads immediately shift back to follow-up:

"Thank you my guy.... I appreciate the heck out of it...."

"1 down for January... just 11 more to go"

The relationship doesn't end at closing — it resets. You've proven you deliver.

+
Pattern 5: Consistency Beats Perfect
▼

In every closed deal thread, there are months of casual back-and-forth before the deal surfaces. The deal comes from being top of mind when the right property appears, not from one magic text.

Consistency beats the perfect message. Stay in touch. The deal will come.

10 — SMS Evaluation Criteria

How to Judge If SMS Works

Cold SMS sequences should be evaluated on: Response rate (did they reply?), Phone conversion (did reply lead to a call?), Tone consistency (one person, not a bot?), Escalation curve (raising urgency without aggression?)

Post-conversation SMS should be evaluated on: Buy box included? Agent name and company included? Sent promptly after call (same day)? Did it match conversation tone?
