Why SMS Works & Forbidden Words
The psychology of text vs email + what words kill deals
3 min ▼

Realtors ignore calls and emails. A casual, human-feeling text gets read. Email disappears. Calls go to voicemail. But a text that feels like it's from a real person, not a company, gets a response. The goal of cold SMS is simple: get them on the phone. Once you're on a call, you pitch. SMS is the opener, not the closer.

Forbidden Words in SMS

NEVER in first cold SMS: "wholesaler", "wholesaling", "wholesale", "assign", "assigning", "assignment", "flip", "fix and flip", "cash buyer", "investor"

Why? In that first cold SMS, these words broadcast exactly what you are before you've earned trust. On a live call or in follow-up texts after the relationship is warm, tone and context matter — "fix and flip" and "cash buyer" are fine. But in cold texts, they trigger red flags and spam filters.

Cold SMS Rules
Short, human, one question — the formula that works
2 min ▼

Cold SMS has strict rules. Violate them and the text feels like a bot or spam blast.

RuleWhy It Matters
Keep it shortOne or two lines max. If they scroll, you lost.
Write like a real personCasual language and occasional emoji. Signals human, not company blast.
Never reveal full pitchSave it for the call. SMS is just the door opener.
One question per textMultiple questions feel like a survey and trigger decline.
Goal: get a yes or replyOnce they respond, you've won. Get them on the phone.

Opening Example 1: "Hey [Name], do you ever work with buyers looking for fixer properties? 👋"

Opening Example 2: "Hey, you still a realtor? Got a program if you ever get investor-friendly stuff."

Opening Example 3: "Hey [Name] — quick question, do you get off-market listings in [area]?"

After they reply "Who is this?" — you've won. They're engaged. Now get them on the phone and pitch.

Post-Call SMS (Mandatory)
Send the "0 Great Talk - Buy Box" template same day
2 min ▼

After every live conversation where the realtor showed interest, send the "0 Great Talk - Buy Box" template same day while the call is fresh. Do not wait until the next morning. This is the gold standard post-call text.

The template includes the buy box in text form, your name, Good Results, and keeps their number saved. It's mandatory because it sets you apart from other investors who ghost after a call.

Why Same Day Matters

The realtor just heard your pitch. Momentum is high. Sending the post-call text while you're top of mind — not tomorrow — keeps energy and prevents them from shopping their property to other investors overnight.

Two Different Follow-Up Cadences
Know the difference: warm vs cold outreach
2 min ▼

Post-call texts and cold outreach cadences are completely different. Do not confuse them.

Cadence TypeTimingPurpose
Post-Call TextSame day or 3 days after callWarm relationship touch. "Hey [Name], hope the week's treating you well." Not a pitch.
Cold Outreach18–21 days apartFor realtors who haven't responded. Standard spacing for cold or silent leads.
CRITICAL DISTINCTION

The 3-day post-call text is a relationship move after a live conversation. The 18–21 day cadence is for cold or unresponsive realtors. These are NOT the same. Do not mix them.

Proven Patterns From Closed Deals
Five sequences that actually work
4 min ▼

Pattern 1: Playful Escalation Sequence — Each text raises emotional stakes slightly without turning aggressive. 1) Friendly check-in: "How goes it [Name]? I hope '26 is off to a killer start for you... Any fresh listings current or coming up?" 2) Playful guilt trip: "Come onnnnn lol... Is this your way of saying I'm not your favorite anymore?" 3) Humor: "Well shoot... Just tell me you hate me if you want to ignore me 🥲" 4) Light persistence: "I'm not trying to be annoying, it's just the squeaky wheel gets the grease lol." Why it works: Escalation without aggression. Realtor is entertained and engaged.

Pattern 2: "Help Me Spend Money" — This exact phrase appears across multiple closed deals: "How's my very favorite realtor in the whole wide world doing? Help me spend some money before the end of [month] lol 🙏" Why it works: Casual, funny, creates urgency without pressure. Reframes the relationship so the realtor is doing YOU a favor.

Pattern 3: Holiday and Personal Messages — Christmas messages, "hope you had a great break" texts, and personal check-ins keep you top of mind during periods with no active deal. Example: "Heyyyy [Name]! I just wanted to reach out and wish you a Happy Christmas and a Merry New Year.. I hope you're having the best time with your family"

Pattern 4: Post-Deal Re-Engagement — After a deal closes, successful threads shift back to follow-up: "Thank you my guy.... I appreciate the heck out of it...." and "1 down for January... just 11 more to go." The relationship doesn't end at closing — it resets.

Pattern 5: Consistency Beats Perfect — In every closed deal thread, there are months of casual back-and-forth before the deal surfaces. The deal comes from being top of mind when the right property appears, not from one magic text. Consistency beats the perfect message.

SMS Evaluation Criteria
How to judge if your SMS is working
2 min ▼

Cold SMS sequences should be evaluated on: Response rate (did they reply?), Phone conversion (did reply lead to a call?), Tone consistency (one person, not a bot?), Escalation curve (raising urgency without aggression?).

Post-conversation SMS should be evaluated on: Buy box included? Agent name and company included? Sent promptly after call (same day)? Did it match conversation tone?

Key Takeaways
SMS is the door opener — calls close the deal
1 min ▼

SMS wins on three counts: It feels human (not a company blast), it gets read (better than email), and it gets replies (better than calls).

The formula is simple: Short + human + one question = response. Once they reply, get them on the phone. The phone is where you pitch and close.

Three timing rules: Post-call SMS same day. Cold cadence every 18–21 days. 3-day warm follow-ups to stay top of mind.

Consistency beats perfection. In closed deal threads, months of casual back-and-forth precede the deal. Stay in touch. Be top of mind when the right property appears.
