Closing, Title & Escrow

01 — The Escrow Process

The Escrow Process

Once under contract, escrow opens at the title company. The escrow/title company acts as a neutral third party that:

Holds earnest money — Your EMD sits in escrow until closing.
Conducts the title search — Reviews public records for liens, encumbrances, and title defects.
Orders payoff letters — Gets figures from any lienholders (mortgage lenders, tax authorities, HOA) for what needs to be paid off at closing.
Prepares closing disclosure — The official document showing all costs, credits, and final numbers.
Facilitates fund transfer — Receives wire from us, sends payoffs to lienholders, transfers remaining proceeds to seller, and records the deed.

Timeline for cash deals: 10–21 days from contract signing to closing (15-day average). No bank approval, no appraisal, no financing contingency. We CAN close on longer timelines if the seller requests it. Lead with speed by default. Never volunteer a longer timeline unless the seller brings it up.

02 — The Title Search

The Title Search

A title search reviews public records to find any issues that could affect our ability to take clear title. Here's what they're looking for:

What Title Companies Search For

Liens: Mortgage liens, tax liens, HOA liens, judgment liens, mechanic's liens
Encumbrances: Easements, deed restrictions, CC&Rs
Ownership issues: Probate complications, estate complications, unclear chain of title
Title defects: Errors in public records, forgeries, undisclosed heirs

Most liens are not deal-killers — they come out of seller's proceeds at closing. Always remember:

Lien TypeWhat It MeansHow It's Handled
Mortgage lienSeller owes money to bankPaid off from seller proceeds at closing
Property tax lienUnpaid property taxesPaid from seller proceeds at closing
HOA lienUnpaid HOA duesPaid from seller proceeds at closing
Judgment lienCourt judgment against sellerPaid from seller proceeds at closing
Mechanic's lienUnpaid contractor workMay need to negotiate; paid at closing
Standard Lien Objection Response
"What's the lien amount? That comes out of seller's proceeds at closing — escrow handles it. Not a deal-killer."
03 — Working With Our Title Companies

Working With Our Title Companies

Texas: Spartan Title (Aaron Krominga). We close 2+ deals/month there — strong relationship.

If a realtor's seller has already opened title elsewhere and incurred costs ($300–400 typically): offer to reimburse those costs by adding them to the offer price. Don't let sunk costs kill a good deal.

Title Company Preference Script
"I have a binder and an incredible relationship with my title company. They've done hundreds of deals with me. Switching costs me tens of thousands in efficiency."
Title Insurance

We purchase title insurance at closing. It protects against undiscovered title defects that arise after closing. This is standard and non-negotiable.

04 — Closing Day (Cash Deal)

Closing Day (Cash Deal)

The sequence:

1. Escrow receives wire transfer from us
2. All payoffs wired to lienholders (mortgages, taxes, HOA, judgments)
3. Remaining proceeds wired to seller
4. Deed recorded in public records
5. Keys exchanged

No bank approval. No appraisal. No financing contingency. If your contractor gives the go-ahead after DD, we cut the check and close.

Earnest Money Rules

Paid to escrow at end of DD/option period (not 3 calendar days from acceptance)
Refundable during DD period if we walk
Becomes non-refundable after DD period ends
Gayden's standard: Make EMD non-refundable after DD as a commitment signal to the seller

05 — Communicating Through DD

Communicating Through DD

The #1 way to permanently destroy a realtor relationship: radio silence during due diligence. Never ghost a realtor.

Set Expectations at Contract Signing

"I'll have an answer for you by [Day 7]. If I find anything significant, I'll let you know immediately. I won't leave you hanging."

Here are three scripts for different scenarios:

Script 1: Proceeding
"Inspection came back fine. We're moving forward as planned. What's the update on [next closing step]?"
Script 2: Terminating
"The inspection findings don't align with our investment criteria. We're walking away cleanly."
Script 3: Still Deciding (Mid-DD Check-In)
"Still working through the inspection findings — will have a clear answer for you by tomorrow morning."

What is title insurance and why do we always buy it?
⌄

Title insurance protects you against claims that arise after closing from title defects that weren't discovered in the title search. Examples: an heir comes forward claiming ownership, a forged deed surfaces, an old judgment wasn't properly satisfied.

We always buy it because real estate is too expensive to risk. The premium is paid once at closing, covers unlimited time, and the policy remains in force even after you resell the property (you can sell it with the policy attached). It's cheap insurance for a big asset.

+
What is a double close and how does it work?
⌄

A double close is two closings that happen on the same day or within hours: (1) You close on the property from the seller, and (2) You immediately close on the property to your end buyer.

Why use it? If a seller objects to the assignment clause in your contract, you can remove it. But then you can't assign — you have to take title yourself and resell it. A double close lets you do that without waiting.

How it works: Your title company coordinates both closings. You wire funds to close on side A (the original purchase), take title for seconds, and then immediately close on side B (your sale). Your end buyer wires in, you pocket the spread, and the seller never knows you were in the middle.

Cost: Usually $1,000–$2,000 in additional title and coordination fees. You only do this if the margin justifies it.
