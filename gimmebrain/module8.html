The Escrow Process Overview
Neutral third party handling funds, title search, and closing logistics
3 min ▼

Once under contract, escrow opens at the title company. The escrow/title company acts as a neutral third party that holds earnest money, conducts the title search, orders payoff letters for any liens, prepares the closing disclosure, and facilitates the actual transfer of funds and title on closing day.

Cash Deal Timeline

10–21 days from contract signing to closing (15-day average). No bank approval, no appraisal, no financing contingency. Lead with speed by default.

Key point: Never volunteer a longer timeline unless the seller brings it up. If the seller requests a longer timeline, we CAN accommodate it — but always lead with our 15-day close as the baseline.

Title Search & What They Find
Liens, encumbrances, ownership issues, and title defects
4 min ▼

A title search reviews public records to find any issues that could affect our ability to take clear title. Title companies search for:

Liens: Mortgage liens, tax liens, HOA liens, judgment liens, mechanic's liens
Encumbrances: Easements, deed restrictions, CC&Rs
Ownership issues: Probate complications, unclear chain of title
Title defects: Errors in public records, forgeries, undisclosed heirs

Key Point

Most liens are NOT deal-killers. They come out of seller's proceeds at closing. You're not paying them — escrow handles it.

Title Insurance
One premium, unlimited protection against post-closing defects
2 min ▼

We purchase title insurance at closing. It protects against undiscovered title defects that arise after closing. This is standard and non-negotiable.

Key Details

One premium, paid once at closing. Covers unlimited time — the policy follows the property forever, not just while you own it. This is cheap insurance for maximum peace of mind.

Earnest Money & Non-Refundability
When EMD is due, when it's refundable, and when it locks in
3 min ▼

Timing: EMD is paid to escrow at the end of the DD/option period (typically days 7-10), NOT 3 calendar days from acceptance. Never let a title company or realtor push EMD to 3 calendar days.

Refundability: EMD is fully refundable if we walk during the due diligence period. Once DD ends, it becomes non-refundable. Gayden uses this non-refundable commitment as a signal to the seller that we're serious.

Remember

The DD period (option period) is typically 7–10 days. At the end of that window, you must decide: proceed or walk. That's when EMD becomes locked in.

Closing Day & Fund Flow
Wire transfer, payoffs, deed recording, keys
2 min ▼

On closing day, escrow executes the following sequence:

1. Escrow receives wire transfer from us
2. All payoffs wired to lienholders (mortgage, taxes, HOA, etc.)
3. Remaining proceeds wired to seller
4. Deed recorded in public records
5. Keys exchanged

Cash Deal Advantage

No appraisal, no bank delays, no contingencies. If the contractor gives the go-ahead after DD, we cut the check and close on schedule.

Post-DD Communication With Realtor
Clear yes/no decision and avoiding relationship destruction
2 min ▼

Always give the realtor a clear answer by the end of the due diligence period. This is non-negotiable for maintaining relationships.

Proceeding: "Inspection came back fine. We're moving forward as planned."
Terminating: "The inspection findings don't align with our investment criteria. We're walking away cleanly."

Most Important Rule

Never ghost during DD. Radio silence is the #1 way to permanently destroy a realtor relationship. A realtor would rather lose the deal than lose certainty about where they stand.
