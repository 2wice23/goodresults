# **\==================================== SECTION 1: SMS STRATEGY OVERVIEW**

SMS outreach serves two completely different purposes depending on where the realtor is in the relationship. These are separate playbooks with separate rules.

**Cold SMS Outreach** — The goal is to provoke a reply. Any reply. These texts go to realtors who have never spoken to us or who have gone silent. The purpose is NOT to find a deal over text — it's to confirm they're a real person and get them on the phone. Tone can be casual, funny, edgy, even slightly annoying. Success \= they reply with anything, even "who is this?"

**Post-Conversation SMS** — The goal is to reinforce the relationship after a live call. Deliver the buy box, leave a professional impression, and stay top of mind. Tone is warm and professional. Success \= they save your number and think of you when a deal comes up.

**Deal-Stage SMS** — Automated texts triggered by pipeline movements (Terms Sent, Offer Out, etc.). These are transactional and should be clear, professional, and action-oriented.

These three categories have different rules for tone, content, and what "good" looks like. Do not score or evaluate them the same way.

# **\==================================== SECTION 2: COLD SMS OUTREACH RULES**

**Purpose:** Get a response. Get them on the phone.

**What's allowed in cold SMS that is NOT allowed on calls:**

* "Are you still a realtor?" — This is a valid provocation tactic in text. It challenges them to reply. Do NOT flag this in SMS the way it gets flagged on phone calls.  
* "I'm new to the area" — Can work as a hook in text to create curiosity. Less damaging than saying it live on a call.  
* Short, incomplete, curiosity-driven messages — These are designed to make them respond, not to pitch.

**Cold SMS sequence principles:**

* First text should feel personal, not like a mass blast  
* Each follow-up should escalate slightly in urgency or humor  
* The sequence should make them feel like a real person is trying to reach them specifically  
* Never reveal the full pitch in text — the goal is to get them on the phone  
* Space texts appropriately — don't blow up their phone in one day

**What to avoid in cold SMS:**

* Anything that sounds like a marketing blast or automated message  
* Long paragraphs explaining what we do  
* Mentioning specific prices or deal terms  
* "Assignment of contract" or "wholesale" — forbidden language applies everywhere  
* Being aggressive, rude, or guilt-tripping

# **\==================================== SECTION 3: POST-CONVERSATION SMS RULES**

**Purpose:** Reinforce the call. Deliver the buy box. Stay top of mind.

**When to send:** Immediately after hanging up from a live conversation.

**What it should include:**

* Agent's name and "Good Results Home Buyers"  
* Quick recap of the buy box (60s-2000s, 1500-2500 sqft, subdivision, any condition)  
* What we avoid (unique/custom homes, acreage)  
* Agent's email  
* Warm but professional tone

**Template model (currently in Close as "0 Great Talk \- Buy Box"):** This is the gold standard post-call text. Every agent should send this after every live conversation where the realtor showed any interest.

**What to avoid:**

* Sending a post-call text that doesn't include the buy box — missed opportunity  
* Being too casual when the call was professional  
* Forgetting to sign off with name and company

# **\==================================== SECTION 4: DEAL-STAGE SMS RULES**

**Purpose:** Keep the realtor informed during active deal progression.

**Templates in use:**

* Terms Sent — triggered when opportunity moves to Send Terms  
* Offer Out — triggered when opportunity moves to Offer Out

**Rules:**

* These are automated and should be reviewed periodically for accuracy  
* They should be clear, professional, and action-oriented  
* Always confirm what was sent and what the next step is  
* Do not include price or sensitive deal terms in SMS

# **\==================================== SECTION 6: PROVEN SMS PATTERNS FROM CLOSED DEALS**

Analysis of all 7 Chicken Dinner (fully closed) deal threads reveals clear patterns in what works. These are not theoretical — these are the exact text patterns that led to real closed deals.

**PATTERN 1: The Playful Escalation Sequence** The following escalation arc appears across multiple closed deals and consistently generates replies:

Step 1 — Friendly check-in: "How goes it \[name\]? I hope '26 is off to a killer start for you... Any fresh listings current or coming up I can check out this week?" Step 2 — Playful guilt trip: "Come onnnnn lol... Is this your way of saying I'm not your favorite anymore?" Step 3 — Humor/fake hurt: "Well shoot... Just tell me you hate me if you want to ignore me 🥲🥲🥲" Step 4 — Light persistence: "I'm not trying to be annoying, it's just the squeaky wheel gets the grease lol. If you want me to lose your number just say so..."

This arc works because each text raises the emotional stakes slightly without ever crossing into aggressive or rude territory. The realtor starts to feel like they owe a response to a real person, not a bot.

**PATTERN 2: "Help Me Spend Money"** This exact phrase appears across multiple Chicken Dinner threads and consistently generates replies: "How's my very favorite realtor in the whole wide world doing? Help me spend some money before the end of \[month\] lol 🙏🏼🙏🏼🙏🏼"

Why it works: It's casual, funny, creates time urgency without pressure, and reframes the relationship so the realtor is doing YOU a favor. One realtor replied "Your follow up is impeccable, definitely warrants a response." That's the proof.

**PATTERN 3: Holiday and Personal Messages** Christmas messages, "hope you had a great break" texts, and personal check-ins appear in every single Chicken Dinner thread. These aren't deal-seeking texts — they're pure relationship maintenance. Examples:

* "Heyyyy \[name\]\! I just wanted to reach and wish you a Happy Christmas and a Merry New Year.. I hope you're having the best time with your family"

These keep the agent top of mind during periods when there's no active deal to discuss. When a deal does come up weeks or months later, the realtor already has a warm association with the agent.

**PATTERN 4: Post-Deal Re-Engagement** After a deal closes, successful threads immediately shift back to the follow-up cadence:

* "Thank you my guy.... I appreciate the heck out of it.... \[transition to next opportunity conversation\]"  
* "1 down for January... just 11 more to go"

The relationship doesn't end at closing — it resets. The realtor should feel like closing one deal together was just the beginning.

**PATTERN 5: Deals Come From Months of Texting, Not One Message** In every Chicken Dinner thread, there are months of casual back-and-forth before any deal surfaces. The Kari thread (Carson City) had months of check-ins before a property came up. The Sonya thread (Elderly Ave) included holiday messages and property discussions that went nowhere — until one didn't. The deal came from being top of mind when the right property appeared, not from one magic text.

# **\==================================== SECTION 7: GOLD STANDARD SMS EXAMPLE — JOE KRUSE → CATHY GENOVESI (FALLEN OAKS DEAL, HOUSTON)**

This thread shows how a single text from Joe led to a deal within minutes.

**January 26 — Gayden's automated follow-up fires:** "How goes it Cathy... Long time no talk... It's Gayden Rosales, this is my new and much more permanent number lol. Hoping the new year has been good to you"

**Same day — Joe follows up personally:** "Hey there, Cathy, long time no talk, this is Joe Kruse. This is my new number if you want to save me. I am still looking for flips if you come across any."

**Cathy replies immediately with a deal:** "I have one at 4311 Fallen Oaks, I just gave the tenant notice to be out by March 1st. Not sure if she will be because she's been a problem, but I don't want to renew her lease."

**What happened next:**

* Joe followed up on Feb 25 to check status  
* Tenant situation delayed the deal (couldn't find a place to live)  
* Joe kept following up patiently through March  
* By March 17 he was asking for the address to go see it  
* March 18 — offer terms sent  
* March 19 — contract executed  
* March 20 — title company info exchanged, deal moving to close

**Why this is gold standard:**

* Joe's text was simple, personal, and direct — no pitch, no pressure  
* He said his name, mentioned it was a new number, asked her to save it  
* He stated what he's looking for in one sentence  
* Cathy responded with a specific property within minutes  
* The deal took patience (tenant delays) but Joe stayed on it with consistent, professional follow-ups  
* Total time from first text to executed contract: less than 2 months

**Key lesson:** The text that sparked the deal was 2 sentences long. The deal itself required 2 months of patient follow-up. Both parts matter.

# **\==================================== SECTION 9: SMS SCORING CRITERIA (DRAFT — TO BE REFINED)**

Cold SMS sequences should be evaluated on:

* Response rate — did the realtor reply at all?  
* Phone conversion — did the reply lead to a live phone call?  
* Tone consistency — does the sequence feel like one person writing, not a bot?  
* Escalation curve — does each text raise urgency/curiosity without being aggressive?

Post-conversation SMS should be evaluated on:

* Was the buy box included?  
* Was the agent's name and company included?  
* Was it sent promptly after the call?  
* Did it match the tone of the conversation?
