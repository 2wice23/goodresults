## SECTION 1: CORE SMS STRATEGY

SMS at Good Results serves three distinct purposes, each with different rules, tone, and evaluation criteria:

1. **Cold SMS Outreach** — Provoke a response from a realtor who hasn't spoken with us. Goal: get them on the phone.
2. **Post-Call Follow-Up** — Reinforce a call that just happened. Goal: make sure they have our buy box saved in their phone.
3. **Deal-Stage SMS** — Move an active deal forward. Goal: keep momentum, handle logistics, close.

A fourth emerging category from the data:

4. **Relationship Maintenance** — Keep warm realtors engaged between deals. Goal: be top of mind when the next deal appears.

---

## SECTION 2: FORBIDDEN LANGUAGE IN SMS

### NEVER Use in ANY SMS:
- "Wholesale" / "wholesaler" / "wholesaling"
- "Assignment of contract" / "assign" / "assigning"

These trigger red flags with realtors and spam filters.

### NEVER Use in Cold SMS (OK on calls):
- "Cash buyer"
- "Investor"
- "Fix and flip" (OK in warm/post-call SMS, never in cold first touch)

### EXCEPTION — SMS Provocation:
- **"Are you still a realtor?"** is a valid cold SMS provocation tactic to get a response
- It challenges their identity and virtually guarantees a reply
- Use sparingly — it's a pattern interrupt, not a greeting

### Additional SMS Rules:
- Never put two questions in one SMS
- Keep cold texts under 25 words when possible
- Never reveal full pitch in text — goal is phone call
- Texts should feel personal, never automated

## SECTION 3: COLD SMS OUTREACH

### Purpose
Get a response. Get them on the phone. That's it.

### Cold SMS Sequence Principles
- First text should feel personal, not like a mass blast
- Each follow-up should escalate slightly in urgency or humor
- Make them feel like a real person is trying to reach them specifically
- Never reveal full pitch in text — goal is to get them on the phone
- Space texts 18–21 days apart with no response
- Don't blow up their phone in one day

### What's Allowed in Cold SMS (NOT on calls):
- "Are you still a realtor?" — Valid provocation tactic
- "I'm new to the area" — Can work as curiosity hook
- Short, incomplete, curiosity-driven messages — Designed to make them respond, not pitch

### Cold SMS Opening Examples
- "Hey [Name], do you ever work with buyers looking for fixer properties? 👋"
- "Hey, you still a realtor? Got a program if you ever get investor-friendly stuff."
- "Hey [Name] — quick question, do you get off-market listings in [area]?"

**After they respond "Who is this?" — you've won.** Now get them on the phone.

## SECTION 4: POST-CALL SMS RULES & TEMPLATES

### Post-Call SMS Timing (CRITICAL)

**Send IMMEDIATELY. ASAP. Same day while call is fresh.**

Do not wait until the next morning. The goal is to reinforce the call while it's still top of mind.

### Post-Call SMS Template (Use This)

**Use the "0 Great Talk - Buy Box" template from Close CRM** — this is the gold standard.

Template structure:
- Thank them for their time
- Include the buy box (60s-2000s, 1500-2500 sqft, subdivision, any condition, cash, fast close)
- What you avoid (unique/custom homes, acreage)
- Your name and company (Good Results Home Buyers)
- Your email
- Warm but professional tone

**Gayden's Real Post-Call Template (from closed deal data):**
"Hey [Name] Appreciate the chat. Quick recap on my buy box: built 60s-early 2000s, 1500-2500ish feet), subdivision-style neighborhood with similar homes nearby. I try to avoid super unique homes/acreage since they're so unpredictable on the resale side. Keep me in the loop if something comes your way. Thank you -Gayden Rosales gayden@goodresults.org"

**Anthony Cruz's Post-Call Template (from Melbourne St deal):**
"Thanks again [Name], appreciate the chat. Keep me on your radar if you get something that we can make some money on! 🙏 Thank you.... -Anthony Cruz Good Results Home Buyers anthony@goodresults.org"

**Joe Kruse's Style:**
"Hey there, [Name], long time no talk, this is Joe Kruse. This is my new number if you want to save me. I am still looking for flips in the Houston area if you come across any."

### Agent Template Requirements

Agents can create their own post-call SMS template in their own style, but it MUST include:
- Agent name
- Buy box (essential criteria)
- Call to action (what happens next)
- Contact info (email at minimum)

## SECTION 5: RELATIONSHIP MAINTENANCE SMS

### Purpose
Stay top of mind with realtors between deals. These are the texts that ensure you're the first call when the next deal surfaces.

### Check-In Patterns (Proven — All From Deal-Producing Threads)

**The "Spend Money" Check-In (Most effective — appears in nearly every closed deal thread):**
- "How're things, anything new I can spend some money on? 🤞🏼🤞🏼"
- "How's my favorite realtor doing?? lol Have you seen anything in the last few weeks I can check out?"
- "How's my very favorite realtor in the whole wide world doing? Help me spend some money before the end of [month] lol 🙏🏼🙏🏼🙏🏼"

Why it works: Casual, funny, creates time urgency without pressure. Reframes so realtor is doing YOU a favor. One realtor replied: "Your follow up is impeccable, definitely warrants a response."

**The Casual Check-In:**
- "Okie dokie... Anything on the horizon?"
- "How goes it brotha... Anything new on [property/area]?"
- "How're things?"

**The "Keep Me in the Loop" Close:**
- "Right on... Well I'd rather see too much, than miss a deal... keep me in the loop if something comes your way in the next monthish? 🤞🙏"
- "Just keep me in the loop. I'm ready to buy whenever it comes time to close"

### Holiday and Personal Messages

Christmas messages, personal check-ins, and "how was your break" texts appear in **every single closed deal thread.** Examples:

- "Heyyyy [name]! I just wanted to reach out and wish you a Happy Christmas and a Merry New Year.. I hope you're having the best time with your family"
- "You don't have to worry about x mas cards... That's so sweet of you though.. You know what would be the best present? Let's do 5 more deals before the year ends"

Why it works: These keep agent top of mind during periods when there's no active deal. When a deal comes up weeks/months later, realtor already has warm association. The Kari Breuer thread shows her wanting to add Gayden to her Christmas card list — that level of personal connection is what produces deals.

After a deal closes, successful threads immediately shift back to follow-up cadence:

- "1 down for January... just 11 more to go"
- "Working on getting it pushed back as we speak... Looking forward to closing this one and building something awesome with your team and mine"

Why it works: Relationship doesn't end at closing — it resets. Realtor feels closing one deal was just the beginning.

## SECTION 6: DEAL-STAGE SMS (NEW — FROM CRM DATA)

### Purpose
Move an active deal forward. Handle logistics. Keep momentum. Close.

### Key Principle: Speed and Brevity

Once a deal is live, transactional texts should be SHORT. The data shows deal-stage messages average under 15 words. Examples from real deals:

- "Okie dokie... Signed"
- "Yes ma'am... We're good to go"
- "Not a problem 🤙"
- "Got it... Just signed"
- "Will get that right now..."
- "Set up for e notary this weekend"

### Offer Sent / Terms Sent
- "Just emailed my terms over for [Address] for [price]. Text me back when you send over the offer and I'll get it signed ASAP"
- "Hey [Name], just sent over our offer terms.... Text Joe or I when you want me to sign. Thanks a bunch, looking forward to making some money together"
- "Wanted to run an offer [Address] by ya, ring me when you get the chance 🤙"

### Contract Negotiation via Text
Real deal data shows significant contract negotiation happens over text. Key patterns:

**Handling Terms Requests:**
- "No worries, I appreciate you keeping me updated 🙏feel free to send as many messages as needed to get this deal done lol 🤙"
- "Not a problem 🤙Have you sent the contract back yet?"

**Handling Closing Timeline Negotiation:**
- When realtor says seller wants 10-day close: Accommodate. Don't push back on speed — it's what we sell.
- When realtor says survey/title needs more time: "Working on getting the closing date pushed back as we speak"

**Contract Detail Coordination:**
- Title company info: Send name, phone, address when asked. Be immediate.
- Addendum requests: "I'll contact them now and make sure they have it all correct with the new addendum"
- Entity name corrections: "The buying entity is to just read Good Results TX LLC, without my personal name in the buyer line"
- Investor disclosure language: Agent should have standard language ready to paste for section 10 investor intent clause

**DocuSign/E-Sign Speed:**
- "Can you send it through Esign? He'll sign off on it right now"
- "I'll have my partner sign off! I really appreciate ya working so late to send it to us🙏"

### Automated Deal-Stage SMS (From Close CRM)

**When you've sent an offer:**
"Hey [Name], just sent over the offer on [Address]. Text me when the contract's coming and we'll get it signed same day."

**Follow-up when offer isn't moving (1 week later):**

## SECTION 10: DEAL PROGRESSION INDICATORS

### Signals a Deal Is Heating Up (Watch For These in Inbound Texts)

**Green light — move fast:**
- Realtor sends property address unprompted
- Realtor sends photos, repair lists, or condition details
- Realtor shares seller motivation ("I'd rather sell to an investor off-market")
- Realtor shares comps ("This other home was sold a little over a year ago for $280k")
- Realtor asks about your terms, title company, or EMD
- Realtor says "I just called you" or "Please text me" — they want to talk NOW

**Yellow — keep nurturing:**
- "Nothing new" / "Nothing flippable at the moment"
- "I wish I had something for you!"
- "I sure will" (to keep in loop request)
- "Pretty quiet on my end"

**Red — back off, check in later:**
- No response after 3+ escalation texts
- "Disliked" or negative reaction to a message
- "Take me off your list" — respect immediately, mark in CRM

## SECTION 12: SMS EVALUATION CRITERIA

### Cold SMS Sequences — Evaluate On:
- **Response rate** — Did the realtor reply at all?
- **Phone conversion** — Did the reply lead to a live phone call?
- **Tone consistency** — Does the sequence feel like one person writing, not a bot?
- **Escalation curve** — Does each text raise urgency/curiosity without being aggressive?

### Post-Conversation SMS — Evaluate On:
- Was the buy box included?
- Was the agent's name and company included?
- Was it sent promptly after the call (IMMEDIATELY/ASAP)?
- Did it match the tone of the conversation?

### Deal-Stage SMS — Evaluate On (NEW):
- **Response time** — How fast did the agent reply to realtor texts during active deal?
- **Deal velocity** — Time from first deal text to executed contract
- **Logistics handling** — Did the agent handle title/addendum/EMD coordination proactively?
- **Momentum maintenance** — Did the agent keep energy up throughout the deal process?

### Relationship Maintenance SMS — Evaluate On:
- **Cadence consistency** — Are check-ins happening every 2-4 weeks?
- **Personalization** — Do texts reference specific past conversations or properties?
- **Tone match** — Does the relationship feel genuine, not transactional?
