# MODULE 4 — POST-CONTRACT SOP

> **Source:** modules-kb.md v2.2 (March 2026) | Good Results Training

---

When a property goes under contract, complete these steps **immediately after signing.** All of this happens the same day or within 24 hours.

---

## The 5-Step Post-Contract Checklist

### Step 1: Fill All Close Fields

Every custom field in the Opportunity must be filled: address, ARV, repair estimate, realtor, purchase price, DD end date, market, property type, systems info, etc. Do this first before moving forward. The Close Opportunity record is the source of truth.

### Step 2: Request Property Photos

- Photos are the priority. Buyers make decisions based on them. Get as many as possible.
- Request from the realtor/seller immediately
- If they can't provide: ask the listing agent (the buyer's agent should earn their commission)
- If still can't get them: talk to Gayden — he may send a $40 runner
- **Never** drive out yourself to take photos. Half a day lost = a deal missed.

### Step 3: Use Deal Page Creator Tool

- Use the Deal Page Creator tool on the GR Hub
- Enter all contract information and property details
- Upload all property photos
- Upload the Purchase Agreement (RPA for Nevada, TREC contract for Texas), any amendments, and supporting documents
- The tool automatically:
  - Builds the deal page with all information
  - Creates standardized documentation in Close CRM
  - Pushes all data to the Close Opportunity record
  - Generates the deal page with property information, contract details, agent information, and systems data

### Step 4: Move Opportunity to Due Diligence Period

Once the contract is signed and accepted, update the opportunity status in Close CRM to **Due Diligence Period**.

> **NOTE:** Due Diligence Period is a Won status. Moving here signals the deal is locked and disposition begins. A task is automatically created for Joe (dispo coordinator).

### Step 5: Document Access Information in Notes

In the Notes section of the opportunity in Close CRM, clearly document:
- **Exact access instructions** for the property
- **Lockbox location and code** (if applicable)
- **Who must be contacted for access** and their contact information
- **Any restrictions on availability** (specific days or times)

Include enough detail so access and property conditions are fully understood even if you are unavailable.

---

## Completion Checklist

Before you are done, confirm every item:

- ☐ All required fields completed in Close CRM
- ☐ Property photos requested from realtor
- ☐ Deal Page Creator tool completed (photos, documents, deal page uploaded)
- ☐ Opportunity status moved to Due Diligence Period
- ☐ Access information clearly documented in Close Notes

---

## What Happens Next (Awareness Only)

Moving to Due Diligence Period triggers a task for Joe (dispo coordinator). The disposition process begins immediately. You are encouraged to maintain communication with the seller/agent, but leadership takes over primary coordination from this point forward.

---

## Non-Negotiables

- If it's not documented, it doesn't exist
- Missing information slows down or kills deals
- Poor organization reduces buyer confidence
- Incomplete access details create friction and delays
- Incomplete fields in Close CRM create confusion across the entire team
