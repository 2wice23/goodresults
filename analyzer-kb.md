## SECTION 2: BUY BOX

- **Year Built:** 1960s through early 2000s
- **Square Footage:** ~2,000 sq ft (give or take 500 — roughly 1,500 to 2,500)
- **Neighborhood:** Subdivision-style, cookie-cutter streets where surrounding homes are similar in age and style
- **Condition:** As-is, distressed, or dated is fine. We do not require repairs.
- **Price:** Flexible — we work backward from the after-repair value (ARV). No hard cap.
- **What We Avoid:** Unique or custom-built homes, rural properties, commercial, or recently renovated homes

## SECTION 4: SCORING CRITERIA

Score each category 1–100:

**1. OPENER QUALITY**
Did the agent clearly state their own name? (This is important — the agent must say their name.) Mentioning the company name is NOT required and should NOT affect the score. Did they provide context for calling? Did they reference a prior conversation specifically (not vaguely)? Did they AVOID asking "are you still a realtor?"

**2. BUY BOX DELIVERY**
Did the agent deliver the buy box verbally on the call? Key elements: subdivision homes, '60s to 2000s, ~2,000 sqft, any condition, cash, fast close. Commission is NOT a required element of the buy box delivery — see category 6. Deferring to email alone = low score. Not delivering at all = very low score.

**3. POSITIONING & LANGUAGE**
Did the agent position as a cash buyer? Did they differentiate from other investors? Did they avoid forbidden language (wholesale, wholesaler, assignment of contract)? "Fix and flip" is perfectly fine — do NOT flag it. Did they avoid undermining credibility ("just moved here," "trying to do," "took time off")?

**4. CONVERSATION QUALITY**
Did the agent let the realtor talk? Did they ask good follow-up questions? Did they avoid answering their own questions or giving easy outs? Did they dig into opportunities when presented instead of punting to email?

**5. NEXT STEP SET**
Did the agent set a specific follow-up? "Mind if I check back in two weeks?" = good. "Keep me posted" = bad. No next step = very bad.

**6. VALUE TO REALTOR (LOW WEIGHT — counts for half the weight of other categories)**
Did the agent make the value proposition clear? This is a bonus category, not a core requirement. Do not score negatively for not mentioning commission. Commission only becomes relevant if the realtor brings it up, or if the agent proactively positions that the offer can be higher since we're self-represented — and if the realtor can double-end the deal, that's a bonus for them. Score this generously unless the agent actively said something that would make the realtor feel like there's no benefit to working with us.

**7. OBJECTION HANDLING**
If any pushback or skepticism arose, did the agent handle it well? Did they reframe or address concerns? Score N/A if no objections came up.

### Pre-Scoring Checks (run these BEFORE scoring any call)

**Voicemail drops must not be scored.** If a call is clearly a voicemail drop with no live conversation, skip the analysis entirely and do not generate a score. This applies regardless of who left the voicemail or what was said. Short calls (under 2 minutes) with a live conversation should still be scored — a good call can happen in 60 seconds.

### Additional Scoring Context

**Call type matters.** Different types of calls get different expectations. A deal discussion is scored differently than a cold open. The goal is always to get to talking about a potential deal, and if no deal exists right now, to build strong rapport so we're the first call when one comes up. Adjust scoring expectations based on call type.

**Buy box delivery on deal discussions.** When a specific property is already being discussed, the buy box delivery score should be reduced in weight. The conversation is past the pitch — the focus should be on gathering property info, running numbers, and moving the deal forward. Do not penalize an agent for not reciting the buy box when both parties are already talking about a specific property.

**"Email me your criteria" is not a close.** When a realtor asks for criteria via email, the agent should still deliver the buy box verbally on the call first. After hanging up, the agent should text the buy box along with a "good talk" message using the template. Email is optional and secondary — the text is the priority follow-up.

**Commission is not mandatory to mention.** Do not score an agent negatively for not mentioning commission. It can be scored as a positive if the agent proactively reminds the realtor they can double-end the deal if both sides agree on price — but it is not a required element. Do not flag it as missing.

**Commission handling (if it comes up).** Agent should use Gayden's framework: love paying agents, fit commission into offer price, can't increase offer for it. If agent agrees to increase offer to cover commission, flag as red flag.

**Tone and energy are not scoring priorities right now.** Do not flag tone, energy level, or delivery style unless something is egregiously off. The current focus is structural: did the agent do the right things in the right order?

**Score every call except internal services.** Score every call to any lead regardless of who is on it — including internal role plays and practice calls between team members. The only exception is calls to leads with a status of "SERVICES" — skip those entirely.

**Skip prank calls, system tests, and junk calls.** If a call has no identifiable agent, no legitimate business interaction, or is clearly a prank or system test, skip it entirely. Do not assign a score or generate an analysis. These are not coaching moments.

### Call-Type-Specific Scoring Context

**Short sale calls:** We work short sales. Score on relationship-building and follow-up commitment, not speed-to-close. Agent should demonstrate patience and communicate our value: early engagement + consistent follow-up.

**Sub2 / loan assumption calls:** Sub2 and loan assumption are different. Sub2 = no qualifying (property goes into trust). Loan assumption = must qualify (DTI, credit, income). If agent confuses these, flag as coaching moment.

**CMA mentions:** If a realtor references a CMA, agent should not rely on it. CMAs use price/sqft and radius searching which are irrelevant. Agent should redirect to proper comp methodology.

**Mold/termite properties:** Neither is a deal-killer for the right price. If agent reflexively rejects, flag as coaching moment.

**Investor-realtor identification:** When a realtor identifies as primarily an investor, agent should route to Gayden. Score positively if routed. Score negatively if agent tries to manage solo.

**Direct-to-seller calls:** New agents should collaborate with Joe on D2S leads. If a new agent handles D2S solo, flag for coaching.

**Title insurance vs closing costs:** Agents must understand the difference. Title insurance costs are only one component of total closing costs. If an agent offers to have sellers "cover all closing costs" when they mean title insurance, this creates misaligned expectations and potential deal issues. Flag if an agent conflates these on a call or in deal terms.

## SECTION 7: COMMON PATTERNS TO FLAG

### Negative Patterns (flag these)

- Asking "are you still a realtor?" as a cold opener — wastes time, triggers suspicion. Can be used occasionally mid-call as provocation.
- Using "wholesale," "wholesaler," or "assignment of contract"
- Saying "just moved here" or "just got here" — undermines credibility
- Saying "took time off" or "took a break from real estate" — undermines credibility
- Saying "me and my partner are trying to do..." — sounds unestablished
- Answering own questions or giving easy outs ("I'm guessing you don't have anything")
- Ending with "keep me posted" as the only next step — passive, puts responsibility on them
- Not delivering the buy box when the realtor has nothing right now — missed seed-planting opportunity
- Punting to email when a realtor says they have properties — dig in on the call

### Positive Patterns (praise these)

- Agent states their name clearly in the first few seconds
- References prior conversation with specific detail (month, topic, property)
- Delivers buy box verbally on every call, even short ones
- Differentiates from other investors ("I'm not just going to submit an offer for the heck of it")
- Runs comps live on the phone
- Floats verbal numbers before any contract
- Mentions commission preservation for the realtor
- Sets specific follow-up timeline ("Can I check back in two weeks?")
- Asks "on market or off market?" as a qualifying question
- Tells the realtor to save their number with a label ("Save me as [Name], investor")
- Lets the realtor talk and asks follow-up questions about their deals
- Keeps tone warm, casual, and low-pressure

**IMPORTANT — SCORING HONESTY:** If a call is genuinely excellent, say so. Do not fabricate weaknesses or coaching points to fill the "What to Tighten" section. Nitpicking audio quality, phone issues, or minor verbal stumbles is not useful coaching. If there is nothing structurally wrong with the call, a short note like "Minor — nothing structurally wrong with this call" is acceptable. Only flag things that would meaningfully change the outcome or quality of the call if done differently.
